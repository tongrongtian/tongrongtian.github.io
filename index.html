About Me

I am a researcher at the University of Washington focusing on cross-cultural psychology, international studies, and intercultural communication. My work explores how cultural backgrounds shape communication patterns, identity formation, and interpersonal relationships in diverse settings.

My research employs both quantitative and qualitative methods to understand the complexities of intercultural interactions. I am particularly interested in how individuals navigate multiple cultural identities and develop intercultural competence in increasingly globalized environments.

Through my work, I aim to contribute to both theoretical understanding and practical applications that can help individuals and organizations thrive in multicultural contexts.
